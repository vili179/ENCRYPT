About ENCRYPT
Dual-layer privacy framework

Digital communication routinely carries highly sensitive information, including personal identifiers, financial records, and confidential evidence, across channels that are subject to observation and interception. Encryption protects the content of these transmissions, yet the presence of encrypted data itself remains exposed, creating identifiable targets for monitoring systems.

ENCRYPT presents a dual-layer privacy protection framework that integrates encryption with image steganography. Encrypted messages are embedded into ordinary images through least significant bit modification, allowing the data to be concealed within visually unchanged media. In this design, encryption secures the message content, and steganography obscures the existence of the communication.

How It Works
01
Encryption
Input message is encrypted using a key (currently XOR-based; AES recommended for production). Output becomes randomized binary data.
02
Steganographic Embedding
Encrypted data is converted into binary and embedded into the Least Significant Bits (LSB) of image pixels. Minimal visual distortion (imperceptible to human vision).
03
Transmission
Output image appears identical to original. Can be transmitted via standard communication channels.
04
Extraction and Decryption
Receiver extracts LSB data from image, reconstructs encrypted message, and applies decryption using shared key.
